Civil Registry Ordinances and Guidelines

In line with our commitment to transparency and public awareness, we are publishing relevant ordinances, circulars, and national policies related to civil registration services. These include legal guidelines for the registration of births, marriages, and deaths, as well as updates on implementing rules and administrative procedures. All citizens are encouraged to stay informed of these regulations to ensure accurate and timely registration of vital events.

Birth Registration Policy

All live births must be registered within 30 days from the date of birth, in accordance with Presidential Decree No. 651. Delayed registration procedures and requirements are also governed by local ordinances and must be complied with accordingly.

Marriage Registration Policy

Marriages must be registered within 15 days after the ceremony. Civil, religious, and customary marriages are covered by the Family Code of the Philippines and related administrative issuances. Ensure submission of all required documents for timely registration.

Death Registration Policy

Deaths must be registered within 30 days from the date of death. A properly accomplished Certificate of Death must be filed by the attending physician, hospital, or funeral service provider. Delayed or unregistered deaths are subject to legal procedures under applicable civil registry laws.
